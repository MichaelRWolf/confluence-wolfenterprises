Letter to David Hawks after SFA 2019

Letter to David Hawks after SFA 2019

David,


Thanks for a great session at SFA. I really do appreciate the Path to Agility, especially since the medium is (part of) the message, namely that this is not a Sage on the Stage experience. It's a Guide on the Side engagement by you with most of the work and learning happening by active engagement by the participants. Bravo.


Great timing on this message, as I was finishing up some notes, and wanted to connect you with the Agile Engineering Fluency diagram that I referenced.


Here's the base diagram -- https://arlobelshee.github.io/AgileEngineeringFluency/Stages_of_practice_map.html? The authors have used "stage" for what I would call team skills, probably because if members don't have the skill then the team is at that _stage_ of learning. These stages are clumped into eras from "Traditional done well" through "Take advantage of being awesome".


Here's one of the most-est base-est (as in fundamental) stage - Build slack in - https://arlobelshee.github.io/AgileEngineeringFluency/Stages_of_practice_map.html?stage=slack


I say slack is critically fundamental because it is a prerequisite for many, many other stages, often all the way into "...being awesome". Here are some stages that do not, at first blush, seem to require slack.

* Smooth flow: https://arlobelshee.github.io/AgileEngineeringFluency/Stages_of_practice_map.html?stage=smooth_flow

* TDD: https://arlobelshee.github.io/AgileEngineeringFluency/Stages_of_practice_map.html?stage=tddesign


I specifically mentioned TDD because many folks want to START with TDD, but it is actually a stage that comes only in the final "era" after many, many, many team and individual skills have been mastered and practiced. That's counter-intuitive to many.


As I also mentioned, this chart was used as the "bones" for the full "flesh" as a playable board game. I have a copy and have been trained to facilitate it. https://www.agilefluency.org/game.php


I've been involved with the Pacific Northwest agile community and, therefore, know two of the game authors (Arlo Belshee and James Shore) as well as others in the Agile Fluency Project (e.g. Diana Larsen). Let me know if you'd like to learn more, get an introduction, or otherwise engage this work. I'll do likewise with respect to Path to Agility.


Best,

Michael


P.S. I made a quick version of the "Team Efficiency" graph I shared with you in class. I'm going to post it on the wall in my office to remind folks that slack is critical. ;-)
