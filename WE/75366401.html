    Avoid the Infamous DevOps Team – John Esser (Day 1 @ 2:15)

    CEO of veracitySolutions.com

    Biggest mistake when implementing DevOps? Making a DevOps TEAM

    DevOps is principles and practices.

    DevOps is not a team or group or person.

    DevOps is not the new name of “Release Engineering” team.

    John showed Innovation Model Spectrum

    • Model S - Simple

    • Model R

    • Model C - Complex

    Showed gears and teams with S and R intermixed. Pull the C out of the S. Get it better, then reintegrate the practices and principles.

    Do not name a team DevOps. Name it for the product (though I disagree with this point).

    Team Topologies is a newly published book. It sounds like a pattern language for team architectures.

    Address culture and behavior in the core (not the special team).

    Make Just Culture Just Your Culture – Dan Barker (Day 1 @ 3:50)

    • Research work of Sidney Dekker
    • Research work of Dr Richard Cook
    • Listen to weekly podcast from Todd Conklin

    Just culture has goals of

    • Trust

    • Learning

    • Accountability.

    Root causes are fairy tails

    Complex systems are always in a state of failure

    In a complex system, all actions are gambles.

    All changes change the future work

    Safety is NOT the absence of accidents.

    Safety is NOT the absence of accidents.

    Safety IS the presence of defenses.

    Language is important.

    • Do not say “I should have ___”.

    • Do say “I did X, expected Y, but saw Z”. It opens up more possibility to learn.

    Blameless post-mortems

    • Provide input before solving problem.

    • Focus on learning now.

    • Action items will come later.

    Asynchronous decisions allow incremental understanding and time to think. Use collaboration tools. Do not race to get an answer in a meeting. Bring it up at a meeting, then make the decision later.

    Role rotations.
