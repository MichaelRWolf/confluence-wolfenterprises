Regarding your request for a learning style book, I am not sure if you are looking for adult (which is my guess) or children as the research shows they do learn differently. So, for adults, a website with some information is https://www.learning-styles-online.com/overview/ They do address more than what academic research typically includes (tactile, visual, auditory, and kinesthetic) and know it has a lot of advertising; but on a basic level it's not bad. Regarding books: (1) Understanding and Facilitating Adult Learning: A Comprehensive Analysis of Principles and Effective Practices by Stephen Brookfield, 1986, and (2) Adult Learning Methods by Michael W. Galbraith, 1990. There are others; but that's a good start. The first one is my topic recommendation. If you're looking for children: Ways of Learning: Learning Theories and Learning Styles in the Classroom by Alan Pritchard, 2005.
