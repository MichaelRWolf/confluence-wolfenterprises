2021-10-27

  • Once upon a time there was developer named Michael.

  • Every day, he would receive patches via email from the other (nearly) identical products that reused the non-central/patched-by-email library. And he would (mostly) apply the patches within a day so that he could add value to the value-add piece that was promised to build on the (smirk) shared code base.

  • One day he called an under-valued former team member.

  • Because of that, Michael was able to facilitate work sessions to form a team out of the group of 6 developers.

  • Because of that, The Team was able to standardize how they organized their directory structure, makefile(5) targets, build process, merge process, update process.

  • Because of that, The Team started finding other ways to collaborate.

  • Because of that, Michael devoted more time to the practices that would eventually become Scrum.

  • Because of that, Michael did not prioritize his developer role.

  • Because of that, Michael’s manager commented that the individual work was not getting done.

  • Because of that, Michael highlighted the productivity of the other 6 Team Members, the speed and quality of the Product increments.

  • Until finally, Michael’s manager made manifest the difference between Michael’s title as developer.

  • And every day after that Michael has been a so-so developer (but a great trainer, Scrum Master, Agile Coach), and has been self-flagellating on his inability to articulate the value of collaborative work habits.
