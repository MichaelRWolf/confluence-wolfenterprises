Richard B. Greene, DBA Business, Golden Gate University
Answered Feb 10, 2017
Originally Answered: How will you develop your leadership skills?

The most important thing before working to develop your leadership skills is to understand what leadership is and realize that true leadership begins with first mastering leading yourself. In the post, Who Are You Leading?, the concept of self-leadership as well as the practice of servant leadership was discussed. Self-leadership is the first step to becoming a more influential leader. After all, if you don‚Äôt have the goods, you can be an effective leader. And, you can‚Äôt give what you don‚Äôt have.

The practice of servant leadership takes the focus off you and puts it on others. If you are a religious or spiritual person, you‚Äôll find many examples of this precept in your faith. It has been said that ‚Äúyou can get anything you want in life if you help enough others get what they want.‚Äù While the issue of servant leadership has been around for thousands of years, it‚Äôs as powerful and relevant today as it was then. The concept of servant leadership is a philosophy that enhances the lives of individuals and builds stronger organizations.

A traditional leadership style is one that is based on the ‚Äúbelief‚Äù that power is bestowed on the leader and the exercise of that power comes from the top. The power exerted is viewed as coming from tradition (those that held it before) and emanating from the position; not from the person themselves.

While this leadership style is widely practiced today, it comes with a whole bevy of problems. New ideas are not always welcome by this type of leader. It‚Äôs usually a ‚Äúmy way or the highway‚Äù type of attitude. This type of leader shuts themselves off from the immediate knowledge of what changes are occurring in the team. Leaders of these teams typically have high turnover because employees grow frustrated at not having input to either their individually assigned tasks or the goals of the team as a whole.

Alternatively, a servant-leader focuses primarily on the growth and well-being of people that that they lead. They don‚Äôt attempt to accumulate power in a traditional top-down leadership style but instead share the power, put the needs of others first, and help people develop and perform as highly as possible. This type of leader realizes that true power emanates from the team and is bestowed upon the leader. It is a position that is earned and one that in practice can be significantly more effective than the common traditional leadership style in practice today.

Robert Greenleaf was the first to coin the phrase ‚Äúservant leadership‚Äù in his 1970 book The Servant as Leader. He said that "the servant-leader is servant first." The desire to serve is a fundamental characteristic of the servant-leader style of leadership. This positioning is not one of subservience; rather that of wanting to help others in a manner that meets the needs of colleagues, customers, and communities.

The servant leadership philosophy has been widely written about and expressed in many contexts. Some of the best known advocates of servant leadership include Ken Blanchard, Stephen Covey, Peter Senge, M. Scott Peck, John Maxwell, Zig Ziglar, Jim Rhon, Margaret Wheatley, Larry Spears, Kent Keith, and Robert Greenleaf.

Becoming an effective servant leader is not something that will happen overnight. But the good news is that anyone can adopt and learn this leadership style. If you‚Äôve made that decision, delve into the writings of the authors listed above. In the meantime, here are three things that will get you started in becoming a greater servant leader.

  1. Be a good listener: You have two ears and only one mouth and you should learn to use them all equally. That means you need to listen more than you talk. Take the time to get to know people. Don‚Äôt just listen to what they are saying but listen to how they are saying it. What emotions are they using? If you are meeting someone in person, what does their body language say? Sometimes just words alone don't really tell you what you need to know about a person and you will need to use your other senses to find out more information. Listening will help you understand the needs of others.
  2. Be Empathetic: Try to put yourself in the other person‚Äôs shoes and think about what may be going on in their life. You really can‚Äôt show empathy unless you know the person you are interacting with and you can make connections to experiences in your own life. Get to know people you do business with‚Äîthey are people too! Remember that people don't really care about you unless they know you care about them.
  3. Have a good heart: Be committed to helping others. Kindness and generosity will make people feel better about you as a person. It will also make you a happier and more positive individual, which can only help contribute to your successes in life.

‚ÄúYou can have everything in life you want, if you will just help enough other people get what they want.‚Äù ‚Äì Zig Ziglar

How Do I Develop Leadership Skills?

‚ÄúThe challenge of leadership is to be strong, but not rude; be kind, but not weak; be bold, but not a bully; be thoughtful, but not lazy; be humble, but not timid; be proud, but not arrogant; have humor, but without folly.‚Äù Jim Rohn

To be an effective leader today, you have to communicate and motivate far more effectively than leaders of the past.

You want to be an exceptional leader?

You have to work hard for it.

You will not magically transform into an influential leader overnight, unless you are the Batman or Iron Man- just kidding.

Nowadays, no company can be run with a chair and a person giving orders to his people.

Those days are long gone.

If that person thinks he and his company can survive in today‚Äôs world with his method, he has more chance of winning the lottery than his wishful thinking.

There are certain factors that play a great role in developing amazing leaders.

If you develop these factors, then your leadership skills are developed automatically.

They work hand in hand. You work on these factors, then your leadership skills will take many steps forward.

Factor #1. Engagement(On Emotional Level)

Have you ever wondered why a company that has developed deep employee‚Äôs engagement is generally more successful than others?

It is not financial gain that is making such kinds of companies thrive and be fruitful than others.

Rather, it is the engagement that is creating satisfaction, commitment and motivation for the people.

If people are happy and engaged on daily basis, then they bring in more productivity.

Ways To Engage:

¬∑ Provide strong leadership.

¬∑ Provide managers who motivate and empower.

¬∑ Listen.

Factor #2. Courage

You have to be rare and act as one, if you want to be recognized as such.

You know why diamonds are so expensive?

It is because they are rare. They are not found easily.

They can only be found in certain parts of the world. If they can be found anywhere just like a common stone, they would not have been this much expensive.

If that was the case, maybe I would have written this answer to you with a pen that is made of Diamonds.

Make yourself a diamond by constantly challenging the way things are done.

That makes you different from others. Not many people do that. Most people in fact accept things as they are.

Developing courage is one of the necessary things that you should do in developing your leadership skills.

You should be willing to stand alone in your convictions if you believe in them(especially if you are alone in your belief).

Habits of Courageous Leaders:

¬∑ They are willing to take risks.

¬∑ They are willing to stand alone if they believe in their convictions.

¬∑They deal with difficult decisions in a timely manner.

¬∑They constantly challenge the way things are done.

Factor #3 Listening

‚ÄúThe most important thing in communication is to hear what is not being said.‚Äù Peter Ducker

It is only when you are actually listening that you are able to identify and understand all the emotions related to a given conversation.

If your body is present while a conversation is going on, but you are not listening then you might as well not be present.

Because ineffective listening makes it impossible to identify and understand the emotions of individuals.

Listening has a huge impact on people more than anything.

If for example, you as a leader delivers a message to your team, seating behind a desk eyes gazing out the window as you speak.

You are fooling yourself, if you think that they are actually listening to you.

They cannot see your face and your eyes, so they are not listening to you. Yes, they hear your voice as you speak, but they are not engaged.

You have put on a barrier to what might have been a successful interaction by your action.

Solutions for Active Listening:

¬∑ Approach every conversation with the goal to learn something.

¬∑ Stop talking and focus on the speaker.

¬∑ Pay attention to your responses (non-verbal communication).

¬∑ Listen for intention.

¬∑ Concentrate.

¬∑ Don‚Äôt Interrupt.

¬∑Keep an open mind.

Tips!

Make listening your full-time job!

Factor #4. Effective communication

‚ÄúIt is the mark of an educated mind to be able to entertain a thought without accepting it.‚Äù Aristotle

You must fight tooth and nail to be an effective communicator.

This concept should not be foreign to the surface of your soul.

If you make yourself part of this factor, not against it, then you will improve the broken edges of your conversations.

Without effective communication, you cannot effectively lead!

Do you want to know what the key element is in an effective communication?

What effective communicators Do:

¬∑ They are inspirational and persuasive.

¬∑ They are honest and authentic in their communications.

¬∑ They connect and engage rather than simply tell.

¬∑ They are genuinely interested in others.

¬∑ They listen attentively and emphatically so that they can understand things from a lot of perspectives.

¬∑ They know that engagement is an important key element in any conversations.

Tips for the Most Important Elements in Effective Communication

¬∑ Listen with the actual desire of trying to understand what the other party is saying.

¬∑ While listening, do not interrupt.

¬∑ Let the speaker know you are listening with small gestures like nodding your head.

¬∑ Pay attention to body language.

¬∑ Try to identify and understand the emotions of the speaker.

Factor #5. Learning

‚ÄúThere is only one corner of the universe you can be certain of improving and that is your own self.‚Äù Aldous Huxley

In order to be a successful leader, there must always be an on-going process of learning and self-improvement all the time.

If you think and believe that what you have learnt so far is enough for you, then you are not on the right path.

Learning never ends.

If you develop this learning habit extensively, then you will acquire a great worthwhile leadership skill.

Make The Followings Your Daily Habit For Constant Learning:

¬∑ Unlearn the unuseful thoughts.

¬∑ Learn from both your successes and failures.

¬∑Reflect upon your practice and challenge yourselves to be the very best that you can be.

¬∑ Enforce yourself for self-improvement all the time.

Factor #6. Team Building

‚ÄúWhat I need is someone who will make me do what I can.‚Äù Ralph Waldo Emerson

The word ‚Äòleadership‚Äô signifies that people are following a certain someone. That someone is leading the group to take them somewhere.

If you want to be that someone, you have to be capable of leading them effectively in the first place.

For that to happen, you have to have an outstanding team-building skills. You must know that you cannot do it alone.

More importantly, you must look (really look) to bring in people that know more than they do and you must identify individual talents.

That is the admirable quality of a leader, bringing out the best in people.

Learn how to guide your team by an example:

Qualities of Effective Team-Builders:

¬∑ They are a facilitator of the team (i.e. they are able to generate healthy discussion and generate consensus).

¬∑ They know that a team performs best when its members have different personalities and styles.

¬∑ They look to bring in people that know more than they do.

¬∑ They know that they are there, to bring out hidden talents that the people themselves do not know.

Tip!

Effective team-builders know that, they cannot do what their team does and their team cannot do what they do.

Factor #7. Training

‚ÄúIt is unwise to be too sure of one‚Äôs own wisdom.‚Äù Mahatma Gandhi

The importance of training really cannot be overemphasized.

Can you think of a strategy that can be used to expand knowledge as effectively as training does?

If you know one, please tell us about it.

Training benefits both the individual and the company at the same time.

Any new skill a company needs and better customer service training's are good for business.

At the same time, the same training's are good for employees in acquiring skills, increasing positions within a company, etc.

If you want to keep your company and the people there always motivated and fresh, then you need to incorporate training's as part of your investment.

If you, as a leader do not start training, how will your team learn from you?

If you think that, just because you are in a higher position and you don‚Äôt need to work on the grass-root level, then you are very much mistaken.

On the way up, You have forgotten to be human.

Benefits of training:

¬∑ Improved collaboration between workers.

¬∑Attracting and retaining great employees.

¬∑ Increased job satisfaction and morale among employees.

¬∑ Increased employee motivation.

¬∑Increased efficiencies in processes, resulting in financial gain.

¬∑Increased capacity to adopt new knowledge.

¬∑ Enhanced company image.

¬∑ Availability of qualified individuals.

¬∑ Making an organization a better place to work.

Factor #8. Humility

‚ÄúThere is no respect for others without humility in one's self.‚Äù Henri Frederic Amiel

This must seem trivial as one looks at the word, but it is an essential quality that you must develop for your leadership skills.

Do you want to know why?

It is very simple actually.

If you are not humble, you are not open to other people‚Äôs ideas period.

It is like a cup which is full to the maximum.

What will happen if you pour on it?

What will be added will be wasted away since there is no enough space in the cup.

You must first make a space in the cup, if you want to add more.

Similarly, you must be humble enough to receive new information from others.

Advantages of Humility:

¬∑ Affects how you work on a team.

¬∑ Makes it possible for you to admit a mistake.

¬∑ Helps you listen.

¬∑ Keeps you quiet when you‚Äôre in a conversation.

¬∑ Enables you to ask for advice.

¬∑ Enables you to keep learning.

¬∑ Enables you to recognize the contributions of others.

¬∑ Enables you to put ego aside and give your best without expecting recognition.

Factor #9. Respect

‚ÄúIf we lose love and self respect for each other, this is how we finally die.‚Äù Maya Angelou

If you want to be respected, you have to be respectful of others to begin with.

Admiration and deep regard for an individual is a great quality you must develop for your leadership skills.

You know there are work places, where you see employees smiling and happy all the time.

In such kinds of situations, it is almost a certainty that respect is a major factor in the culture of the work place.

Do These Things to Show Respect:

¬∑Use people‚Äôs ideas to change or improve something.

¬∑Say you‚Äôre sorry when you hurt someone.

¬∑ Clean up your own mistakes.

¬∑Communicate in a polite manner.

¬∑ Never cut off another person.

¬∑Share everything.

¬∑Include your team in meetings, discussions and more importantly decisions.

Factor #10. Praise and Recognition

‚ÄúEverybody likes a compliment.‚Äù Abraham Lincoln

You must not be quick to find faults in people. All of us make mistakes. We learn from them and we go on.

As a leader, you must create the culture of praising and recognizing every individual in your team.

If you tell them the positives before their mistakes, they are more likely to listen to you. Even when you tell them their mistakes, tell them in the manner of positive-reinforcement.

Best Practices of Praising:

¬∑ Be surprising.

¬∑ Say Thank-you.

¬∑ Write a sincere Thank-you note.

¬∑ Be specific in your praise.

¬∑ Match the praise to the effort.
