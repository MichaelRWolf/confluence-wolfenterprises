BLOOM : Audio Cleaning SOP

Created by Wendy R Wolf on 2024-11-05

PART 1: Katie selects the audio & mark original file with a C. Then send
This allows us to know which files are being worked on

  • Please keep name and start the whole line with a “C.”

  • Date should be yearmonthnumberdaynumber.etc

  • 20201016.Peace Meditation Healing 2 the thing and followup
    becomes C.20201016.Peace Meditation Healing 2 the thing and followup

PART 2: Directions for audio cleaners

  1. Katie will send you a linked hightail folder which has your “assigned” recordings. Click the link and choose your first audio to clean.

  2. Open your named worksheet from this folder -https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1St5gLWn-pWOxM9HJVSeRdsklXc4u6Rab?usp=sharing - Jill uses Jill’s, Katie-Katie’s, etc.

  3. For each recording:

    1. Add a new tab/sheet at the bottom of file by pressing + at the bottom left

    2. Name that new sheet the name of the workshop or class you are cleaning

    3. Copy and paste the green headers from the “Ex. HH1 class1” sheet onto your newly added sheet - You’ll use this to structure your notes as you listen to the audio for the first time through. Please read through the example of notes so you have an idea which direction to go.

    4. Listen through the audio (can be done on hightail) and in your newly created sheet - markdown timestamps with notes of what’s discussed, if the noted section will be kept or cut, any tools focused on, if there is a segment that could be pulled out as a stand alone mini-lesson, any testimonials (“this work has changed my life”) that could be used for marketing and who to ask for permission of use. These are all listed in the green headers as reminders. Use the Ex HH1 class1 as a guide of what we are looking for.

    5. Once the first listen through and notes are completed, upload the audio into your audio cutter. We suggest using Audacity - it’s free and the program Katie is most familiar with if you need help. There are tons of tutorials on youtube to help you out, as well. Here are a few to get you started:

      1. How to import an audio into audacity

        https://youtu.be/EGocD5a12-w

        (Make sure to download the original audio from hightail to your computer or a flashdrive first then import into Audacity.)

      2. This video is a quick run through of trimming... 

        https://youtu.be/drmKIXcZM6k

        It doesn't include how to cut out something in the middle though, so when you're ready to do that, watch this next one.

      3. Another good trimming video
        lots of keyboard shortcuts, suggest a little notepad to write those down.

        https://youtu.be/VoBVcaMEJxo

    6. Now that you have finished cutting/cleaning the audio:

      1. Save the audio to your computer

      2. Please rename newly cleaned file by changing the C. to an FL. at the beginning of the file name

        1. Example: C.20201016.Peace Meditation Healing becomes - FL.20201016.Peace Meditation Healing

        2. (FL=Final Library)

        3. If renaming the file is a challenge, reach out to Katie and she can change it once it is uploaded to hightail.

      3. With the newly named file, upload to the appropriate folder at this link:

        1. https://spaces.hightail.com/storage/hightail/Njg1ODYzNjI=/MSAtIGNsZWFuZWQgTGlicmFyeSAtIHdvcmtzaG9wcywgY2xhc3NlcyBldGMgLSByZWFkeSBmb3IgU3Vic2NyaXB0aW9u
          Username: wendyrwolf@att.net
          Password: BlessedAreYou13!

        2. If you don’t see a folder that makes sense - reach out to Katie for clarification

        3. How To Upload in Hightail:

          1. Click on the “+ add file” on the right hand side

          2. Select file(s) to upload and click open

            2. You should see the newly added file load up and (briefly) show a checkmark on the left hand side.

    7. Now that you have finished cutting and uploading the new file, please complete the “Info for Master Library” sheet of your worksheet. An example has been provided - We suggest copying and pasting timestamps and details from your original notes to quicken the process. (We expect these time stamps to be from the original audio, not the newly cut one.)

      Using the wrap text button shown above will help to make the cells the correct size for your information.
