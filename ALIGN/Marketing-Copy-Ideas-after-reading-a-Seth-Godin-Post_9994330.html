from Seth Godin  *Some problems are easier to sell*

We all have a Genuis Zone - stuff we are naturally good-at (and something we aren’t naturally inclined to, as well)

Directly related:
Each of us has something in this world that only we can do - a difference only we can make - a Calling.

The question is - are we operating here?
How much of our natural abilities are we enjoying? sharing with our world?
Are we aligned with our Unique Way in this world.

When we aren’t it can be painful - in our soul and in our life.
When we aren’t, life can feel to easy - not challenging enough - or too hard - too challenging and we can’t ever win.
This is because we are playing the wrong game!

This can feel like boring (Jack & Diane)
It can seem like we are missing the boat - how come everyone else is succeeding, and I’m not?
This can look like being off track - Dr Bruce used to say, when we are driving our car, and we get off the road, we start hitting things - mailboxes, trees, houses… When we are off-track in our lives, it can be similar.

Everyone is here with a Reason, a Purpose, something that fulfills us and serves in the symphony of our world.
And if we will not play our role in the orchestra - everyone loses our contribution, and most of all WE miss our opportunity.
This is not a one-time shot, the door never closes on this - until we step off the stage - but the sooner we consciously invest, the more fulfilling our life can be.

If you are fulfilled, making a difference, utilizing your Genius, satisfied with the Legacy that you are building, enjoying the fruit of your life - within you and in your world.
Hallelujah! Bless you. Keep on.

For the rest of us, I would like to encourage that
living a Life a Meaning, is worth our attention, our intention, our Choice.

Our society wants to focus on quick-fixes, success defined in very narrow ways, dying with lots of toys…
But most of this seems to me to be a lot of empty calories.

What if we lived in a Dance with life that was Creative,
that brought our the Best in those around us,
that inspired us to live a challenge big enough to get us over all the resistance, the friction that keeps us from living our dreams.

We live with missing the mark in our lives, its often just background noise - until:
- our birthday, especially one ending n a 0, reminds us of where we are and who we wanted to be…
- a health crises, death of a loved-one, something intense happens in our world…
- something shifts, possibilities open - we enter a wonderful relationship, a child is born in our tribe, our finances improve, our nest empties, we retire…

But every day that goes by, if we are just getting by, living a mundane life - it is a tragedy.
And the symptoms increase as the days, years, decades go by.

but we often don’t notice,
or if we do notice we call it depression and take some drugs, we call it mid-lie crisis and buy a fast car
and eventually we die unfulfilled, not savoring, not manifesting, not lighting up the world - with the life we could have lived.

We have a choice about this - everyone of us - not just the rich and famous - not just those of us with moderate means and no one paying attention. Every adult has a choice, at every moment. We read stories about elderly,, wealthy, queer, of color… or not - doing amazing things, having incredible lives, making a huge difference. Why not us?

Saying yes to the Call, to a Life of Purpose, to the Way before us,
is a challenge.
It is called the Hero’s Journey for a reason:

It will cost us - in one way or another: time, money, energy.
It will cost us our pride - we won’t always know the way, and some days the dragon will win.
It will cost us our certainty and our righteousness - we will Learn and Shift, and so will how we perceive and relate-to our world and ourselves.

Regardless of the difficulty, the challenge to our sense of self and the many potential pitfalls,
Living the Life of our Dreams, is worth it.

Anything else is shortsighted.
The whole time I am typing this, I am hearing
The Un-Examined Life is Not Worth Living
