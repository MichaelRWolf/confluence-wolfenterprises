Your “BIG WHY” statement

Before you get going, I think it’s really important to get a clear vision of WHY you are deciding to write a book for your business.

Sure, it’s likely to boost your profile and make you a lot more money...

And all of that is GREAT, really!

That said, over the years, I’ve found that for those ideas to really take root and have personal meaning, you’ll want to GROUND them for yourself in your personal reality.

In other words, how will having this book done REALLY change your life?

Let me give you a couple of examples.

When I wrote my first book, The Very Cool Life Code, I did so because our first child was on the way and it was time to take my business to another level of profitability.

Thinking of my new family, wanting to be “the provider” was a powerful motivator.

Couple this with the fact I’d invested $15K in a coaching program right before I learned Karin was pregnant, and I had a very clear “WHY” in front of me.

I needed the money! :)

And so, I got focused and serious about writing my book, and in fewer than 90 days, I was holding my first copy.

I went on to leverage the book into high end coaching programs (from $5 to $15K), membership programs, and live events.



In other words, with my personal BIG WHY in place, I made sure my investment paid off (and it has many times over).

One more brief example...

In 2015, I did a complete overhaul of The Book in 90 Days Program to make it conform to my definition of “great.”

And yes, this required a lot of focus and time.

I also had the vision to share this on a much larger scale and to once again take my business to a whole different plane of financial success.

Again, I needed a BIG WHY for myself to ground this project and keep me on track.

In short, both my kids would be entering a nature-based



private school in the fall (a place that fits our personal values), and quite simply I needed to pay for the tuition bill (x 2!) for the year. I didn’t want my wife and I having the stress of wondering how we were going to manage this.

And so, this BIG WHY focused me and in the first month of offering the new version of the program, I made $22K.

Having this kind of money (and the vision and plan to have this revenue show up every month) has been a life-changing experience.

In short, my family has enough money where it’s not something we spend any time worrying about anymore.

We get the things that we desire. We live where we want to live. We take vacations. We don’t fret over the prices on the menu... You get the idea...

So before you begin, I encourage you to spend some time getting clear on your personal BIG WHY for deciding to write this book.

Using the document in this folder, answer this question for yourself and post it somewhere to remind you when you need a little nudge to get back on track!

THE 45-MINUTE FOCUS CALENDAR

Being successful in realizing this vision of holding your book in your hands is so simple it can be summed up in three words.

“Show up consistently.”

That’s really all there is to this.

And of course, while this is simple, it’s not always easy.

On a practical level, if you make the decision to show up for this project 45 minutes a day, for at least 5 days a week, you will be in really great shape.

You don’t need to even be writing every day — some days just thinking is enough.

To monitor your progress, print out the FOCUS CALENDAR. The day you begin this program, start

 

tracking the days each week where you can track the days that you’ve put in at least 45 minutes.

Keep this document close where you can see it daily and begin crossing off at least 5 days per week.

When you hit 65 days, chances are good your book will be in great shape.
