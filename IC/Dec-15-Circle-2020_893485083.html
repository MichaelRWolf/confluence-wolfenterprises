3. Compassionate Accountability

small steps over time - IS THE WAY

4 parts to Compassionate Accountability

1 - Clarify Vision - Clear but not too precise where you want to go and results you want - from everyday and soul consciousness. When you are clear on your goals, and what you are hoping those goals will provide for you, then you can make clear commitments.

2 - Regular Review - path curves and not always aimed quite right, so review to course-correct - therefore take small steps. Pick a structure for regular review (you can ask Ryan to review your work weekly. You can even create daily check-in with others if you want.)
Accountability happens when you take the time to look backwards, and compare what you did with what you had committed to do.
It’s as simple as that.
Included in this is less frequent review of big goals, strategic vision, and why you’re doing the whole thing in the first place.

3 - Discernment: Look for the full picture. Understanding what happened & didn’t happen

  • was scope bigger than I thought? unreasonable commitment? priorities shifted? personal issue?

Pause and look more deeply each time you miss a commitment, to see what’s really needed.

4 - Keep it Fresh - Shake it up - Dance your goals and actions, not trudge.
Neuroscience says: we are programmed to pay attention to change and newness - It’s a survival trait, that keeps us scanning our environment for threats. Too routine becomes less important, make it new again.
