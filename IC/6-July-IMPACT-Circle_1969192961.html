Choosing the Destination
Before getting to the details of your offer, first discover the destination.

  1. Take a moment to drop into soul focus.
    Let that deepen for a few breaths, and let go of concerns about “getting it right.”

  2. Picturing a particular client of yours, what destination are they trying to reach?

  3. In their journey towards this destination, what problem(s) are they running into?
    What’s keeping them from reaching this destination?

  4. Now ask in your heart to be shown, what is a further destination you want for them? What can you see beyond their destination?


BBBB

As you map out the journey towards your clients’ destination,
keep in mind these four things:
(1) Results that the client wants;
(2) Satisfaction for the client in working with your offer;
(3) Repeat business so they keep journeying with you;
(4) Referrals to other clients who need to travel to the same destination.

Beginning to Craft the Offer

What Do You Need in Your Travel Pack?

When you start to list out deliverables
you want to provide in your package,
that’s when your good old friend Overwhelm, Resistance, Invalidation… appear

The next question to ask then is:
what do you need in order to provide those deliverables?

Looking at the list of features you will be providing,
what resources do you need to be able to provide those features?

Being a business owner is like playing dominoes
– your customers are leaning on you, and so what are you leaning on?

There are two categories of resources you will need.

The first category is Obvious resources.
Obvious resource include
time in your schedule to provide the “sessions,”
design support for your workbook or handouts,
technical help with the App you are having them utilize

The second category is the Non-Obvious.
Things that end up on the non-obvious list may include
plenty of sleep,
open and unscheduled time in your work day to foster creativity.
Mentors and colleagues who can help you trouble-shoot.
Education and training to expand your own learning so you can provide more expertise.

A Special Note on TLC

I get stuck all the time before releasing a new product or offering.
It’s happened to me enough times that I can recognize it easily now.

It’s a tender and vulnerable moment to offer something out to the world,
and that gives us pause.
Take that pause.
Just don’t get STOPPED


one of my non-obvious resources

I need is to be witnessed.
I need Support & Encouragement & Validation

I need people who love me, and whom I trust,
to witness me and what I’m offering.

That deep sense of being seen and accepted helps me also.
When that need around witnessing is met,
I can connect to my strength again and move forward with my offer.
