Her copy is fun, refreshing, and in line with the kinds of ways we look at work and coaching. I feel like she strike a good balance between working without over-structuring (or rather, seeing structure as the reason we don’t go forward) and honoring ourselves in all the messiness that shows up in real life.

She has a coaching program, as well as a podcast.

Here’s the email sequences that I’ve seen from her so far:

Weird Rule #1: Feeling like shit is not a problem.Weird Rule #1: Feeling like shit is GOOD.

Conventional coach marketing says: Be aligned! Be in sufficiency! Dance on a sea of unicorn farts, and inspire people to want the same! 

I say: Feeling like absolute fucking garbage a lot of the time... IS your superpower.

So much of what fucks us up when we try to get a business up and running... is how often we just feel really fucking terrible. 

All of the self-doubt. The fear. The shame. The imposter syndrome, the exhaustion, the recurrent trauma responses.

"If I'm swimming in a sea of yuck, how the hell am I qualified to offer people help with their lives?"

Be honest. How often has that brought you down?

Well, guess what? You might have been programmed by what I call “Feel Good Fascism.”

Feel Good Fascism says, feeling good is morally superior to feeling bad.

Feel Good Fascism says, feeling bad is "less than," and it makes you weak, faulty, defective somehow.

"I mean, look... If you drank more green juice, self-coached correctly, did more exercise and managed your time better, you'd probably feel better, so you've got nobody else to blame for feeling bad.

You just have to stop being a lazy, negative, trash-eating asshole, okay?"

Feel Good Fascism is the most insidiously hidden form of ableism.

Got trauma - personal, familial or ancestral?

Did horrible things happen to you or your people?

Got an ADHD, autistic, depression-prone, anxiety-prone, or highly-sensitive brain?

“Please go away with all of your messy, inconvenient, impolite wounding. We only talk about the weather, #slayingit, Instagram analytics over here.”

That’s the messaging we’ve been subject to all our lives. 

Feel Good Fascism is Orwellian.

It says, our ultimate aim in life is to exist in a serotonin-oxytocin-dopamine-spiked state... because it doesn't threaten the status quo. 

Any other state serves no purpose except to distract us from the simplicity of living a shallow, unquestioning, alchemy-free, lap-dog-like existence.

When there's no serotonin-oxytocin-dopamine, your condition is something to be overcome and changed as fast as possible.

(The way we life coaches are complicit in this... is, sometimes consciously and other times unconsciously, we've normalized pain-avoidance in the name of "healing" and "growth" and "evolution.")

Feel Good Fascism turns feeling good into a credential.

"See? I feel so good. This is who I am. Here's an Instagram picture of me to prove it -- looking blissed out in a pool in a 5-star resort on a tropical island that was formerly colonized land, and now where 1% of the white people own 99% of the capital! Look at my shiny life and smile in my hot bikini! I am totally qualified to lead you to live #yourbestlife!"

"And you over there, sitting in your stained pajamas, typing on your old ass laptop that keeps crashing, in your apartment in that dodgy zip code! Please, just stop it now. You're making the rest of us life coaches look bad."

Feel Good Fascism is the opposite of love.

It's fear.

Fear of our own complexity... and fear of paradox... which is really fear of the true, vast, profound, immeasurable greatness of the human spirit.

It flinches away from darkness because it thinks it cannot survive touching it.

But it is wrong.

We can survive it. In fact, we are made to survive it.

Not only are we meant to survive it, we are meant to be FORMED and FORGED FROM IT. It is meant to show us who we truly are. And we do not have to be afraid of it.

We are life coaches. And we will not be complicit in upholding a political, economic or spiritual order based on fear. We can and will do, so, so, so much better.

Feeling good is not a precondition for your authority as a life coach. 

If you’re feeling shitty, it means you’re living the shit out of life, paying attention, and growing. 

Feeling like shit IS your credential.

Coach from it, lead from it, and market from it.

xo,

Simone

Weird Rule #2: No mo' planning.

Weird Rule #2: Free yourself from regimented plans.

Conventional coach marketing says: If you want to be a grown-up who makes great things happen, write it down, make a list, schedule it in advance, and follow the plan.

I say: Genius flows from organic flow, surprises, and liminal spaces. 

I don't follow a schedule.

I don't abide by a planner. They look pretty, but in the end, usually just make me feel like a failure.

I never plan my launches. I mean, my plan amounts to (1) decide what I'm offering, and (2) tell people about it. But that's about it.

I don't have one of those neat lists, detailing what is "due" when. I just have a feeling I should start to work on something. And I do. for the most part. If I don't meet a "deadline", too bad. 

I don't write my emails in advance. And I often send them at 3:29 AM, as inspiration strikes.

I have ADHD.

My brain doesn't work in a way where lists and plans and schedules make sense. In fact, my brain staunchly rebels against them.

And I still have a world-changing business that makes piles of money. 

It's possible to have a different brain - one that does NOT thrive with organization and planning-in-advance and lists - and STILL create everything you want.

​Maybe your thing isn't ADHD. Maybe it's clinically diagnosed anxiety. Or autism. Or a chronic illness that leaves you with fewer spoons than most. 

Whatever it is, your brain, and the unique way it works, is not a liability. It's your biggest asset.

Instead of planners and protocols and schedules, here is what I do have, instead, which works way better. And it will for you, too.

Self-trust.

Friendship with my work.

Devotion to my mission.

Love for those I serve.

The willingness to let it be a little messy.

These.... are enough.

And these.... you can have.

Whatever brain you have.

And they are enough to get you to fully express your purpose and get you to fully booked -- and even to 6-figures, 7-figures, and beyond.

​It's important for us folks who are "different" to lead the way.

Fuck how we're "supposed" to be.

Let's become proof that we can be outrageously successful and happy -- in our own terms.

And be a beacon for those who don't yet know what's possible.

xoxo,

Simone

Weird Rule #3: BE the niche

Weird Rule #3: BE the niche.

Conventional coach marketing says: Know exactly the demographic of who you serve, and what results you help them get. Have a "I help" statement. 

I say: When you’re genuinely lit up from the inside, the right people will hunt you down... and you don't have to know who they are in advance.

I push my clients hard to craft a clear and consistent message. Because a clear and consistent message is what STICKS to people's brains.

And unless something sticks to your brain, it's actually difficult to take action to seek the help you want.

Here's how people often misunderstand me when I talk about that.

When I say "clear and consistent message," some folks think I'm forcing them into a straight-and-narrow niche-box.... and an elevator pitch.
​
Nothing could be further from the truth.

Screw elevator pitches. Screw "I help" statements. And screw straight-and-narrow niche-boxes.

A niche is nothing more than that which allows people to distinguish you from others.

Here's a good starting place: your face. Your face is a good niche. It allows other people to distinguish you. I mean, right?

I'm completely serious. What if your face is enough to let your Right People know that you can help them?

Like... here's my face. And it's enough.

And when it comes to talking about your work, the best niche is whatever the fuck lights YOU up.

And I don't care how unconventional, out-of-the-box, or mish-mash-y you think it is.

My client Jacqueline leads people to create dazzling reinventions using... housework and theatre.

WTF? Is that a thing?

YES. You see, she’s a coach... and a housework goddess, and former actress. She brings all of her talents and passions together into one coaching offer that her people just can’t get enough of, and her business is thriving. 

You can do the same. I don't give a crap if you think your niche doesn't make sense.

Neither do your people, by the way.

Astrology-reading social justice warrior coach?

A coach for sensitive weirdos who love art and want more out of life?

Amazing. There are people in a line waiting to fall in love with you.

They're so interested in your unique take on things. In fact, they're hungry for it.

They want to front-row seats on the way YOUR brain processes things. Because that - above all else - is what's healing, refreshing, illuminating, and compelling for them.

Can you dig it?

There are sooooo many coaches with unique visions whose *thing* CANNOT be reduced to a corporate-y elevator pitch.

The difference between those among them who sign tons of clients... and those who don't... is just one thing:

They commit to BELIEVING that who they are, how their brain works, and what lights them up is ENOUGH.

It's about time you believed it, too.

xoxo,

Simone

Weird Rule #4: Only the “HELL YES” people.

Weird Rule #4: Speak only to the “HELL YES” people.

Conventional coach marketing says: Overcome objections. Anticipate their fears. Push on their pain points. Study human psychology to hack consumer behavior. 

I say: When you talk to people who are a “hell yes” on exactly who you are, you get to just be yourself and don’t have to do any convincing ever to create all the clients you want.

Let me tell you a quick story. I was sitting at a bar in Harlem a few years ago, having drinks with a dude who was sort of a friend, but also had ulterior motives.

I mean. I’m fine with ulterior motives. I don’t mind being wanted! (This was a long time before Simonefound Mr. Dreamboat the Husband, to be clear!)

Anyway, there was some flirtation going on, and he was cute enough.

And here’s what he said over a bourbon. “Simone, the thing with you is that you’re really smart. And most men don’t like a woman to be smarter than them. It intimidates them.”
​

**CUE MY EYEBROW RAISED ALL THE WAY TO HIGH HEAVEN**

Then he says — “but not me. I like a woman who’s smart. It turns me on.”

Now — POP QUIZ TIME!

What should Simone’s reaction have been?

(A) “oh wow, unlike other guys, this one is NOT turned off by my intelligence! How refreshing! It’s a winner! You can take me home now, sir!”

OR

(B) “ummmmmm..... first of all, my intelligence doesn’t exist for your penis, and second of all, what kind of DINGBATS do you surround yourself with that you think ‘the average man’ is turned off by a woman’s brain?”

....... drumroll please.......

If you said (B), you are in agreement with 2016 Simone!

Okay, you might be like “WELL DUH.” But...

So many coaches are marketing to, and hoping for, the equivalent of this guy!

The client who likes SOME aspect of you... but with caveats.

And potentially for the wrong reasons.
​
Marketing to the wrong client looks like....

... editing yourself to make sure they don't think you're 'too much'.

(“I’m posting this offer two days in a row... I hope they don't think it's annoying!!" )

This looks like catering to anyone who will come and validate you in some way.

("Well, i've got some moms in the group who seem to be engaging... but they're sensitive about politics so I'll shut my mouth about it!" )

Letting your messaging be influenced by anyone who **kinda-sorta** likes what you're doing.

("My friend said she really loves my message but thinks my photos should be more professional, so I better go hire a photographer....?" )

Folks who follow you and seem to like you EVEN THOUGH they're content to keep pushing those ️ and  buttons without ever reaching out to you for a consult.

("So inspiring!"..... and that's the end of their action-taking, ever. )
​
STOP. MARKETING. TO. THESE. PEOPLE!!!

THESE. ARE. NOT. YOUR. PEOPLE!!!!

Listen. In your love life, you deserve someone who is a HELL YES on you. Exactly the way you are. ZERO modifications necessary.

That person exists. And you'll find them a lot faster if you BELIEVE that they exist, first. Right?

Same thing in your BUSINESS.

You deserve clients who are a HELL YES on you. Exactly the way you are. ZERO modifications necessary.

And just like with romantic partners... it’s up to YOU to believe that there are clients out there who want to hire you NOW...

... NOT when:

  • You're certified

  • You have a perfect niche statement

  • You're less anxious

  • You're 10 lbs thinner

  • Your house is better organized

  • You're just the right amount of 'outspoken'...

​
... but NOW.

They want to send you money, exactly the way you are.

Will you please let them?

Weird Rule #5: Love = Money

Weird Rule #5: Love is what gives you power.

Conventional coach marketing says: Know exactly what problem you’re solving, and market the solution.

I say: Just be yourself, and love more. Love is the best magnetic force. Love draws your soulmate clients in, and Love fills in all the gaps. 

BEING LOVING is the best marketing strategy.

Now I have millions of dollars in revenue to prove it... but I didn't always know what this meant. 

You see, I have a very fiery, fierce nature. It got me in a lot of trouble.

Fiery and fierce WITHOUT love = snarky, self-righteous, and dictatorial

It was not how I wanted to be in the world. So I figured out a different way to be.

I decided to try something different. I decided to add love to the mix.

Fiery and fierce WITH love = sharp, innovative, galvanizing

Now, THAT was a recipe that worked.

Here's how to find the recipe that works for YOU.

I also talk a lot about the importance of BEING YOUR UNIQUE SELF. The thing that makes you stand out in a crowd.

After all, nobody has your precise combination of history and personality. You are one of a kind, one-in-seven-billion.

BUT being unique IN AND OF ITSELF doesn't do any good if you're not loving on some part of yourself.

Notice how, the way most people react to themselves (their history, personality, interests, strengths and weaknesses)... is only with SELECTIVE love?

All the shiny, normal, socially acceptable stuff gets plenty of sunshine and airtime... and the rest get shoved under a moldy rug in the basement? and la la la let's pretend it doesn't exist?

Baby, you gotta add love to ALL of it... because, here's the winning formula:

UNIQUENESS + LOVE = POWER

  • When you pour love on the thing-you've-been-trying-to-hide (like your 3 a.m. panic attacks, your imperfect marriage, or your childhood trauma from leaving a dogmatic religion), your presence becomes medicinal for others. Because it gives them permission to show up and take up space, too.

  • When you pour love on your REAL stories -- especially the one that are hard for you to tell -- and TELL them, you become inspiring AF. Because most people are hiding from their own.

  • When you pour love on your "flaws", your less-socially-acceptable 'edges,' they become your competitive advantage. (example: My ADHD makes me a fast-acting powerhouse. My laziness makes me a master of efficient shortcuts.)

  • When you bring love wherever you go, and ASSUME love in every single social interaction, you become a force of nature. People cannot help but gravitate towards you, because EVERYONE wants to be around that.

​

No amount of algorithm-hacking, fancy photo-shoot-ing, or copy-tweaking is as important as LOVE.

For your self.

And for others.

Giving it before you get it.

Pouring it on the light AND the darkness.

On the order AND the chaos.

The best thing about love is that it's available to all of us... in infinite quantities... free of charge.

The more we access, the more there is that can flow through us.

And the more we access, the more we become the kind of person clients have to follow, stalk, and eventually pay to work with.

xo,

Simone

Weird Rule #6: Be angry as f*ck.

Weird Rule #6: Anger is your best messaging strategy.

Conventional coach marketing says: Inspire people! Attune to a higher frequency! Positive vibes only! 

I say: Be mad as fuck. And tell people why you’re mad.

Anger gets a bad rap.

Especially those of us life coaches. We love healing, wholeness, potential, transcendence. ALL the nice ideas with a halo on top!

And... we might think anger is only appropriate when directed toward Donald Trump.

Y'all. There are OTHER uses for anger.

Anger, in its clean form, is powerful medicine.

Anger is fire.

Fire is fuel, and it cooks your food and provides us with heat and energy.... without which, we DIE.

And a WHOLE LOT OF ANGER is missing in life coaches' marketing.

It's like we're all walking around in some new-age dystopia, where everyone is drugged and walking around like zombies, spewing things like:

  • Embrace your authentic self!

  • Your thoughts create your reality!

  • Impossible is just a story!

  • Heal your mind, heal the world!

​

WTF is this generic shit?

​BLAHHHHHHHhhhhh. Are you asleep yet? I got so bored I scrolled past myself.

Where is the bite? The edge? The challenge? The righteous anger?

Is anyone actually SAYING something worth saying, or are we just in a daze, spewing cliches and calling it "service" and "marketing"?

Don't get me wrong. All of those ideas are... like... true.

But we all deserve better than to be constantly told "Impossible is just a story." Yeah, so fucking what?

You know what marketing I want to hear?

I want to hear about how the media FUCKS with women 50+ by subtly signaling that you don't really exist if you're post-menopause. And even smart women just swallow that shit up, and give up on their own sexuality. Tight little low-cut dress? Pass.

I want to hear about how ambitious professionals treat chronic fatigue and anxiety as NORMAL and par for the course. After all, it's all so complicated and it's a tough world out there and everyone is busy and tired and needs Klonopin and whiskey to get through, right? No. That is fucking BULLSHIT.

I want to hear about how the entire "marriage therapy" industry profits off telling couples that the way to happiness is "compromise". GAHHHH that shit actually makes my blood boil, and relationship coaching ain't even my niche!

You know the difference between a fully-booked coach and an underbooked coach?

The underbooked coach puts on a smiley face and presents through bright + sunny instagram filters, while her angst is festering inside.

The fully-booked coach treats anger -- loving, righteous, creative anger -- as a friend and ally, and puts it out in front in service of the greater good.

She keeps her anger close. And she uses it as fuel to innovate, inspire, and galvanize people to action.

Got some anger?

Of course you do.

Fan those embers.

Present it to people -- with love.

Let it burn and swallow up bullshit on its way.

xo, 

Simone
