Impact Circle : Trade: meet 1-1 with other participants

Created by Wendy R Wolf, last modified on 2021-03-16

If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together 
-Author Unknown


Grow your Network & Support System: coffee date/ video chat
– to build/ deepen relationships, get feedback about your latest and greatest, brainstorm with a buddy, practice your skills, trade healing, brainstorming, leaning-into each others skills and strengths, coaching, mentoring, etc… whatever suits you

Please be mindful of each person giving and receiving a similar amount of time, to receive energy and attention - share, get help, etc.
I like to use a timer and have one person at a time be the ‘client/receiver. healer’- but its up to you.

Please plan with one person per week, or as you can, you can plan:
a coffee date - 15 min
a lunch date - 30 min
a healing/ coaching trade - an hour or more
As you please - relative to the time/energy you want to invest generally, or that week.

this is our current Trade Schedule

If you don’t want to play in this part of the game, please let me know ASAP, and I will update this schedule.

IF your buddy isn’t available a particular week, please do it anyway! on your own.
I encourage you to invest that week in a ‘Solo Entrepreneur Date'
- just like an Artist’s date, because you as an entrepreneur are an artist in business, as well as in life.

yes, this is supposed to be
fun,
and deepening into relationship with yourself (and heaven/earth)
but it is also really useful investment in your work: it is FEEDING the WELL.

Here’s what Julia Cameron (creator of The Artist’s Way) has to say:
“The Artist Date is …festive, solo expedition to explore something that interests you. The Artist Date need not be overtly “artistic” —think mischief more than mastery. Artist Dates fire up the imagination. They spark whimsy. They encourage play. Since art is about the play of ideas, they feed our creative work by replenishing our inner well of images and inspiration. When choosing an Artist Date, it is good to ask yourself, “what sounds fun?” — and then allow yourself to try it.”
