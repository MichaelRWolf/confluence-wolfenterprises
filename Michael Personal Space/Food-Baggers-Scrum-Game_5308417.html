Michael's Personal Space : Food Baggers Scrum Game

Created by Michael R. Wolf, last modified on 2019-02-05

Preparation

  • Gather pens, paper, tape to create Information Radiators
  • Create User Story Card, visible to all
  • Create Score Board, visible to all
    • Sprints across top.  Number them 1, 2, 3, 4
    • Team spaces down side.  Do NOT put team names in.  Let teams name themselves.
    • Each square is divided in half.  Top half will have estimate.  Bottom half will have actual.
  • Label a "Delivery Zone".

Introduce the Game

Have each team choose a name and post it to the score board.

Discuss User Stories, and show visible display.  As the customer, you say WHAT result you want, and the team is responsible to choose HOW to perform the work.  The Team is encouraged to have discussions with Product Owner (or Customer) to learn more.

Review timing.

Explain ceremonies:

  • Sprint Planning (and silently implicit daily standup) – Teams will discuss what needs to be done, and how the members will work together to achieve the goal.  They must produce an estimate and post it to the Score Board before beginning sprint work.
  • Sprint Retrospective – Prompt them with the simplest retro that could work:  What went well?  What didn't go so well?  What should we continue doing?  ... start doing?  ... stop doing?  It's probably too heavy-handed to have them write these down; it'll come up in the final debrief.

Timing

Divide time to allow 4-6 sprints, including introduction and debrief.  Debrief is NOT optional.  It is where most learning happens.  Do not skimp on debrief time.  It is the ROI.  Having invested in the work, reap the reward of the learning.  Make it fun and safe, and deep learning will happen.  This is experiential learning at its core, and remember "All Mammals Learn by Playing".

  • 5-7 minute Introduction of the game/exercise/contest
  • 10-15 minute debrief
  • 20-30 minute sprints
    • 5 minute sprint planning
    • 5 minute sprint retrospective
    • remaining time sprint execution


Run the Contest

Watch how teams are self-organizing.  Take notes (written or mental) on what they do, and also how they make that decision.

If you see them struggle, ask if they want coaching.  If so, encourage them to find their own answer rather than giving them your answer.  Use your judgement, though, so that they don't get bogged down in process.

Make time visible and audible.  Announce these events.  Post times to the wall.  Use a visible timer.  Use a (gentle) bell.  Create a sense of urgency without instilling a panic.

  • Sprint Planning start time
  • Sprint start time
  • Sprint Retrospective time

Debrief the Experience

Start with very open-ended questions:

  • What did you experience?
  • What did you learn?

Let them explore the experience in a conversation so that they own their own learning.  Probe with other questions as the discussions slow down.

  • What did you learn about how to do the work? ... how to improve the work?  ... how to communicate?   ... how to lead or follow?
  • Was Sprint Planning helpful?
  • Was Sprint Retrospective helpful?
  • What is the right mix of pre-work planning and in-work learning?
  • Did your estimates get better?
  • Did estimating the work help improve the PRODUCT? ... the PROCESS? ... the TEAM?
  • What was the benefit of having multiple sprints?  (i.e. How did looking forward (at sprint planning) or backward (at sprint retrospective) affect things?)

User Stories

Scrum teams often get work defined in a "User Story" which must state

  • WHO will benefit
  • WHAT will be delivered
  • WHY this is important
  • HOW it will be verified

They have often taken a common form of

AS A <role>
I WANT <feature/benefit>
SO THAT <business value>
AND I'll KNOW IT'S DONE WHEN:
    - Acceptance Criteria 1
    - Acceptance Criteria 2

Too many "feature requests" only include the WHAT without thinking about what value it will bring, who it will benefit, and how completion will be verified. 


For this exercise, see how much more context is provided around the WHAT (i.e. "weekly food bags")?

AS A Food Bank
I WANT weekly food bags
SO THAT we can distribute them to customers/guests
I'LL KNOW IT'S DONE WHEN
 * Filled bags are located in Delivery Zone
 * All incoming food is bagged
 * Work area is cleaned up for future iterations
 * Bag count is recorded in <specific location>
 * What else???
