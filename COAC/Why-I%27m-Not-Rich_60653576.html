Coaching : Why I'm Not Rich

Created by Michael R. Wolf, last modified on 2019-06-21

At our 2019-06-17 Meeting (Frank + Michael), Frank asked two difficult questions.  They are answered (way) below, but on top, my take-aways from pondering the questions.

Summary

Take Aways

I see my own perspectives as all so weak.  Victem-y.  Small.

But that's how I feel, and what I got in touch with when I asked the question.  It feels small and grubby, but it's there for a reason.

Different Futures

If the future were to be different form the past, I'd have to

  • Believe that I have value, and can share (i.e. exchange) it.
  • Believe that large-mindedness can win over small-mindedness.
  • Believe that my current framing isn't the only framing.
  • Believe that my creative/curious energy and ability to connect is bigger than the average bear (hey, Boo Boo).  (BTW... attending the local PMI really helped with this!)

The Questions

Why Am I Not Rich?

  • Although I didn't have a good answer during our meeting, I got a sense of what is in the way as I started researching jobs and imagining myself in them.  Here's a 5 Why's run down:
  • Getting jobs is annoying
  • Fitting into the job description is awkward
  • Ultimately, people are small.  They let trivial things get in the way (i.e. sitting on the floor or letting A/V connections be a shared team responsibility).
  • Small-mindedness gets in the way.  Sand ruins the gears.  The machine grinds to a halt.
  • Small-mindedness eventually wins.
  • Trying to make a difference doesn't work.
  • Why bother???!!!   It's simpler to lower my expectations (and live on less) than to push that hard only to have the system tear it apart


  • It takes a team to get anything large done.
  • Teams don't last.
  • There is no such thing as a humane organization.
  • Winning in a non-humane organization is non-humane.  Zero sum.  Selling out to get ahead.
  • That's not a fun way to invest a life.

So, I see my assumptions:

  • What I see as trivial, other people see as important.
  • Vice versa.

What's My Relationship with Money?

I don't focus on it.  I assume that it will follow if I provide value.  Focusing on money is the root of all evil.  I try to focus on something beyond that – value.

... And then I don't believe that I have value or can add value.  I'm never (quite yet) good enough.
