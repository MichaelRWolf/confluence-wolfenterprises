Coaching : Market Analysis: Technical Agile Coach in NoVa

Created by Michael R. Wolf, last modified on 2019-06-21

As followup from 2019-06-17 Meeting (Frank + Michael), I researched technical coaching opportunities in NoVa (near Mom, Steve, and Tamara).

What I learned about the market

There are not as many "Technical Agile Coach" opportunities as I thought.  Still, there are many listings that hint at technical practices, even though they're often the last item on the list.  Some of the DevOps coach and trainer positions require the kinds of current proficiency that I remember having as I transitioned from a developer to a trainer.  When dealing with techies (it's my opinion that) it's critical to know the technology very deeply, thus the "Up to date programming skills" requirement.  I don't know these technologies very deeply, so I go to my self-talk of "I'm not good enough, and I can't add value.".  I have some validation of this based on a conversation I had recently with someone at LitheSpeed - he said that they were looking for people to pair-program with their clients as a mode of training.  Doing this kind of technical leading takes having some chops up.

For further exploration:

  • Consultancies: Converos, AgileTrailblazers, Eliassen, LitheSpeed
  • Companies:  Capital One, Fannie Mae.  (But only if something is compelling.  Otherwise, they're big financial organizations much like Fidelity, so that's a down-tick)

What I learned about me

  • I am more motivated to learn technology to work with techies than I am to learn vision alignment techniques to work with product and strategy folks.  This insight is huge. It points more toward "what I know" (about technology) than "who I be" (with people).  It says that I still like being the smartest guy in the room, or at least being in a room of continually smarter-ing people instead of continually relating-er people.  I'd rather learn more with people than to relate better with them.  It's about bonding over "what we each know well" instead of "how well we know each other".
  • Some realizations about the shape of the problem and the role:
    • It's (perversely or paradoxically) comforting to imagine myself in a large company with other experts so that I won't get caught not knowing everything.
    • Agile transformations are done on/to/with large companies.
    • I am allergic to large company culture.
    • Large company culture has an immune system with antibodies that reject me.
    • Even if I'm in a small consultancy, I'll be on site at a large company.
